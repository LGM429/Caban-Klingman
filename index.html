Councilmember Cabán Outspends Klingman in Clear Reelection Win

Queens City Council Race sees progressive defend District 22 seat, which represents Astoria and parts of East Elmhurst and Long Island City, amid low turnout.

By Leo G. Miranda

Nov. 30th, 2023

Queens City Councilmember Tiffany Cabán, Democrat, decidedly defended her seat against Republican Challenger Kelly Klingman earlier this month.

Cabán, a member of Democratic Socialists of America (DSA), outspent Klingman, a self-styled Reagan moderate, almost three to one, according to public figures. Cabán received close to 70% of the vote—a few percentage points better than her last election in 2021.

Cabán's reelection comes at a time when DSA politicians have come under fire from some Democrats for their stance on the Israel-Hamas War.

“The DSA has made itself radioactive in New York politics,” Democratic Congressman Ritchie Torres told Politico in October, just weeks before elections were held.

Movers in the City's GOP have also put DSA members in their crosshairs.

In the spring, 2021 GOP mayoral candidate Curtis Sliwa spearheaded the creation of Ronald Reagan Republican Club in Astoria to challenge progressives and democratic socialists like Cabán and AOC by allying with moderates.

In Astoria, Klingman, a real estate agent, had hoped to garner voters from conservatives, moderates and Democrats who found Cabán’s policies too far left. Debbie Rigas, a liberal voter, said she supported Klingman because Cabán had not been effective addressing quality-of-life issues. “She’s not visible,” Rigas said.

In a phone interview as early voting opened, Klingman said that out of 100,000 registered voters in the district, 64,000 are Democrats. Of those, 75% are moderate, “old-school, Kennedy Democrats,” said Klingman. “They don’t like either fringe.”

Klingman was unsuccessful in her aim to build a broad coalition. The Cabán campaign outraised her more than three to one, leaning heavily on out-of-district donations.

John Krinsky, a political science professor at City College, said he did not see this as a competitive council race. “Progressives are fairly strong in their districts,” he said.

This election cycle there were almost 7000 fewer votes cast in District 22 compared to 2021, the last time the City Council elections were held.
